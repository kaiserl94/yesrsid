Victorian Era

Through the re-imagined space of the Victorian Era, Neologisms puts the accent on replacing textiles sourced from all over the world due to the growth of the global trade in the mid-1800s, with local, more accessible fabrics. The heavy, expensive curtains and upholstery of old were designed to last a lifetime but often employed materials, chemicals and manufacturing processes that produced considerable waste. The modern materials used in this project, in contrast, are locally-sourced and made of organic and recycled materials produced through non-toxic, chemical-free manufacturing processes which make them more sustainable but also reduces their lifespan.

3D printed Thermoplastic polyurethane

After considering MIT’s most recent developments in the field of 3D printed fabrics, we chose to use a 3D printed TPU for the walls of this design. The MIT researchers experimented with many materials until they discovered that basic TPU provides the best results. They created a very effective, flexible, and strong, but soft fabric.

Thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU) is rigid – making it easier to use for 3D printing. It’s durable and can retain its elasticity in the cold. TPU is degradable, non-toxic and made without the use of plasticizers. There is no DMF residue in the final products and the raw materials used in the production process can be recycled.

Recycled polyester

Knoll considers sustainability to be related to the decisions that shape the daily lives of the community. The design, manufacturing and management decisions Knoll makes are tightly connected with environmental, economic, and social factors.

Abacus upholstery resembles wool. However, it is made of 100 percent recycled polyester sourced from both post-consumer (soda bottles) and post-industrial (production scraps) materials. The products contain either 75% natural or bio-based materials, or a combination of pre and post-consumer recycled content making up at least 50% of its composition.

Linen

Crate&Barrel produce eco-friendly products by minimizing petroleum-based foam and using more energy-efficient alternatives such as soy and corn-based foam and fibers, as well as partially recycled steel. The company uses a specialty facility that recycles all fiber and fabric remnants for alternative applications with no harmful liquid or solid waste.

Crate&Barrel Avery Linen Double Drum Pendant Light is made of linen which is one of the most environmentally sustainable fibers to produce. The flax used to make linen can be grown anywhere in the world, while the manufacturing process itself is simple, chemical-free and requires very little water.

Recycled Plastic Bottles

Shaw Contract has been turning clear plastic bottles into carpet fiber for more than a decade. In 2003 they found a way to incorporate harder-to-use green plastic bottles into some of their products and, in 2019, produced a line of carpets that is 100% recyclable.

The Striation rug is a PVC-free carpet designed to be separated into component materials for carpet-to-carpet recycling. Each tile is labelled with a toll-free number that customers can call and arrange to have used tiles picked up for recycling.
